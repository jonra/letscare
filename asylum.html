Asylum

What to do first after arriving in Norway

Register as soon as possible with a state authority. This can be the police or the immigration authorities. Tell them you wish to apply for asylum. The officers will arrange accommodation for you in a nearby reception center. Here you will receive information about the asylum procedure in Norway.

You will also receive a Certificate of Registration as an Asylum seeker. This document is very important. It serves as identification and enables you to receive the benefits given to asylum seekers such as food, beverage, accommodation and clothing as well as articles for health care and personal hygiene. Asylum Status

Some asylum seekers receive their decision shortly after the interview, while others have to wait for several months. Once your asylum interview is over, the interviewer will tell you how long it is likely to take to receive an answer to your application. If the UDI needs more information in your case, you will be notified. You will also be notified when your application has been considered.

Rejection

If you are staying in Norway without a legal residence permit, you must return to your home country or another country where you have a residence permit.
